# **ðŸ”¹ 1. Main Bowl (\"o bowl grande\")**

This is the centerpiece of Orla --- a large, modern concrete bowl with
deep and shallow sections.

**Features:**

- Deep end with real vert feel

- Shallow pocket for learning coping work

- Perfectly smooth walls

- Wide hips connecting the pockets

- Metal coping all around

**Good for:**

- Carving at speed

- Airs and floaty frontside/backside grinds

- Carve grinds, 50-50s, smiths

- Spine-transfer-style tricks between pockets

- Pumping lines without ever pushing

If you like transition skating or want to learn it, this bowl is one of
the best in the entire city.

- Perfect for: rock to fakie, axle stalls, early grab airs, slash grinds

This is an ideal warm-up transition zone.

- Learning coping tricks in a safe way

- Practicing rock to fakie, slash grinds, and lip tricks

- Wallrides in some angled sections

Ideal for warming up before going into the deeper sections.

# **ðŸ”¹ 6. Street Section (Smaller but Efficient)**

Orla is primarily a flow/transition park, but it *does* include a small
street area.

Expect:

- **Ledges** (low to medium height)

- **Manual pads\**

- **A couple of low rails/flatbars\**

- **Small bank-to-ledge setups\**

This area is flowy and compact --- great for:

- Noseslides, crooks, 50-50s

- Learning switch tricks

- Linking flat + ledge lines

- Manual tricks with lots of runway

If you love street but also want transition, Orla gives you both worlds.

These make Orla a super playful park.

It's flat, fast, and clean --- perfect for long warmup lines.
